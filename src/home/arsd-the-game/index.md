# aRSD - a Reason to Sing and Dance - The Game

## Overview

### The Name

aRSD is an acronym for a Reason to Sing and Dance. It is pronounced by spelling the letters R, S, and D one after the other. The a letter at the beginning is silent.

### What is aRSD?

What is this supposedly new game about? At the core, it is a game where you guess song titles and artists by listening to music. What is new about it is the use of modern music streaming services to deliver a deep and broad experience which anyone can strongly connect to. The technology is combined with a rule set optimized for a single purpose: fun. This text details how the person running the game should orchestrate the people, the music, the accessories, and the rules to achieve the maximum fun effect.

### How is it different?

aRSD as it name says is just a reason to sing and dance. Singing here is an extension of listening while enjoying the music. Music ties itself to our memories. By listening we feel. By singing we relive. By dancing we commune. The trivia aspect is highly diluted to serve as a concrete vehicle for producing fun. The competitive aspect is an illusion managed by the fun master. You will listen to your most beloved songs, sing along, dance if you have the guts led by trivia and spurred by small joyful victories.

### What do you need to play?

The following minimum elements are required to play aRSD:

- a music system capable of playing songs from a list, it must be loud enough for lyrics to be heard while people are talking. The view of the song name and artist currently playing must be limited to a single person. The music system must be capable of jumping to the next song in a list at any point and to display how much time has elapsed since the beginning of a song.
- access to a large library of songs which you can set up in a list for playing in sequence
- a mechanism to pause a playing song in less than a second by manual intervention
- three people

The following optional elements will greatly improve the experience:

- access to a universal song library where you can access most songs ever created
multiple tokens of different type to keep track of points. For example: poker chips.
- two devices producing different noise to act as buzzers. For example: a plastic horn and a hand bell.
- a music system loud enough to incite dancing
- five people

To play the game to its fullest you will want:

- a very loud music system equipped with a sub-woofer
- a music system with multiple song lists support including list copying and merging
- buzzer devices for each player
- drinks and snacks to sustain a long game session
- more than five people to a maximum of about 12

### Getting Started

The quickest way to get a game of aRSD going is to use the public Spotify song lists found in the song list. You can make copies and tweak them to your taste. If you use a different universal music service, like Google Play or Apple Music, the lists are available in text format as well. You need someone to take the role of fun master. He will need to at least an hour reading this document and the Fun Master guide. He will likely also need to spend a few hours preparing song lists matching the taste of the people he will be playing with.

## Terminology

### Fun Master and Players

One of the person playing aRSD has a special role called fun master. The fun master is responsible for running the game and ensuring everyone has the best possible time. The fun master needs to prepare the song lists before game sessions. The fun master has the prerogative to overrule or change any of the rules to improve the game experience. As is name suggests, his only success criteria is how much fun people have. He should be dedicated to achieving this goal even when faced with opposition. The greater happiness should prevail over individual satisfaction.

Every other person playing the game is a player. Players should avoid looking at the song lists prepared by the fun master. Although players might be tempted to look at the song lists, the lack of surprise when encountering songs will reduce their enjoyment of the game. It will also give them an advantage over players who have not seen the song lists.

### Teams

Players are grouped in teams of one or more persons. Each team should generally be limited to up to four players. Keeping team size to four or less people gives all players the chance to participate. The recommended number of players per team is three. You need a minimum of two teams.

There can be more than two teams when playing with many people. The teams will alternate throughout the game in pair of two. Team pairing is decided by the fun master.

It is in everyone’s interest to separate the players into balanced teams but ultimately it is the fun master’s responsibility to do so. Teams are balanced when it is hard to predict who is going to win. The constant suspense is a key fun factor. It is recommended for the fun master to move or switch players between teams at any point throughout the game to improve balance.

### Primary and Secondary Answers

During the game, songs from a list are played by the fun master. Players try to quickly guess the title or the artist of the song playing. If the song has many artists, a single one suffice for the answer. The word artist is equivalent to singer, composer, author, band, or group depending on the song. The team with the fastest correct answer scores a point. The first guess given by a player is the primary answer. Giving a wrong primary answer incurs a point lost and gives the other team the opportunity to earn points. If the primary answer is correct, the player can give secondary answer. Multiple guesses are allowed for the secondary answer. If any of the secondary answer is correct, the team scores an additional point. If the primary answer was the title, then the secondary answer must be artists. If the primary answer was one of the artist, then the secondary answer must be a title.

If a primary answer is given by a player of a team and it is deemed incorrect by the fun master, a counter primary answer and a counter secondary answer become available to the other team to score points.

### Answer Time

The answer time is a time limit into a song before which the players must give their answers. The answer time is one minute for songs longer than two minutes. For songs less than two minutes, the answer time is half of the total song length. When answer time is less than a minute for a song, the fun master should tell the players what it is before the song starts playing.

### Points

Points are awarded to teams when they give correct answers. Points can be taken away for certain incorrect answers. A team cannot have less than zero points. If a team loose a point when it currently has none, the other team should be awarded a point instead. When points are awarded to teams, the fun master will give the players tokens representing the number of points. The points must be displayed in the open for all players and the fun master to see.

## Game Flow

Although this game flow section contains some intricate aRSD rules and guide lines, the game is actually easy to play in real life without a need for players to read this text. The fun master can explain the key elements in about fives minutes to a group of players assuming he understands the game well.

All times given in this game flow section are approximate. The fun master will adjust the times constantly to improve the game experience.

Because the purpose of the game is to have fun, the fun master can be lenient when determining if a primary answer or secondary answer is correct. Players must accept his judgement with happy smiles.

### Game Turn

A game turn is played around a single song. A turn is divided into multiple ordered steps. When reading steps, follow the ordered actions sequence. The action sequence goes from sentence to sentence then step to step or moves to a different step when stated.

#### Step 1 - Turn Start

A turn start with a song selected from a song list. The song is at the start and paused. The fun master counts down 3-2-1. This countdown is a reminder to the players that the next steps are coming thus players should get ready. The fun master un-pauses the current song. Go to Step 2 - Identify Song.

#### Step 2 - Identify Song
The players must try to identify either the title of the song playing or one of its artist. Players should choose whichever one they think has the most chance to be correct as their primary answer and have the other one be their secondary answer. Go to Step 3 - Indicate Intention.

#### Step 3 - Indicate Intention
When a player wants to give his answers, he indicates his intention to the fun master. The first player to give an indication is chosen*.

- If buzzers are used, the player makes noise or gestures with it.
- If buzzers are not used or not every player has one, raising a hand, jumping up, shouting, or any other gesture can be accepted by the fun master as an indication.

* If players from both teams indicate their intention at the same time, the player in the team with the less points is chosen. What is considered the same time, as Einstein demonstrated, is relative and subject to the fun master judgment.

If a player indicates his intention to give answers within one minute, go to Step 4 - Primary Answer else 

go to Step 8 - No Point.

#### Step 4 - Primary Answer

When a player indicates his intention to give answers, the fun master immediately pauses the song. The player must give his primary answer within three seconds. Other players can suggest primary and secondary answers. Although not to his advantage, a player from the other team will often suggest answers. The fun master will ensure only a few such suggestions occur usually as a reaction of being about to give the answers.

If the player does not give his primary answer immediately, the fun master counts down 3-2-1 in three seconds. The fun master can wait before starting the countdown. After the countdown the fun master un-pauses the song. The player must give his primary answer before the song starts.

If the player did not give a primary answer, go to Step 6 - Counter Primary Answer. 

The fun master assesses the correctness of the player’s primary answer:
- correct primary answer: go to Step 5 - Secondary Answer.
- incorrect primary answer: the team of the player looses one point. Go to Step 6 - Counter Primary Answer. 
 
#### Step 5 - Secondary Answer

The player who gave the primary answer can give the secondary answer without incurring a penalty if it is incorrect. In addition, the player can try multiple secondary answers. The fun master can accept secondary answers from anyone part of the players team. If the player does not give his secondary answer immediately, the fun master counts down 3-2-1 in three seconds. The fun master can wait before starting the countdown. After the countdown the fun master un-pauses the song. The player must give his secondary answer before the song starts else his secondary answer is considered incorrect.

The fun master assesses the correctness of the player’s secondary answer.  If the player gives a correct secondary answer go to Step 10 - Award Two Points else go to Step 6 - Counter primary Answer.

#### Step 6 - Counter Primary Answer

The fun master un-pauses the song.

Players from the team that did not give the primary answer can now try to give a counter primary answer. They must indicate their intention to give the counter primary answer as in Step 3 - Indicate Intention or they can wait for a minute to have elapsed where the fun master pauses the song and ask if a player wants to give answers. There is no game reason to give an indication before the minute has elapsed other than for the player to show (off) his mastery of the game. If a player wants to give answers, this step continues else go to Step 8 - No Point.

The counter primary answer is handled as in Step 4 - Primary Answer except that go to:
- Step 5 - Secondary Answer is replaced by Step 7 - Counter Secondary Answer
- Step 6 - Counter Primary Answer is replaced by Step 8 - No Point

#### Step 7 - Counter Secondary Answer

The counter secondary answer is handled as in Step 5 - Secondary Answer except that if the if the player gives an icorrect counter secondary answer go to Step 9 - Award One Point.

#### Step 8 - No Point

The fun master states the song's title and artists. Go to Step 11 - Song listening.

#### Step 9 - Award One Point

One point is awarded to the team of the player gave the correct, optionally counter, primary answer. The fun master says “one point” while showing his index to the players. Go to Step 11 - Song Listening.

#### Step 10 - Award Two Points
Two points are awarded to the team of the player who gave the correct, optionally counter, primary and secondary answers. The fun master says “two points” while showing his index and his middle finger. Go to Step 11 - Song Listening.

#### Step 11 - Song Listening

The fun master un-pauses the current song. After one minute has elapsed, the fun master goes to Step 12 - End of Turn. The fun master can let the song play longer based on the player's appreciation. The more singing and dancing, the longer he will let the song play.

#### Step 12 - End of Turn

Fun master checks if the round, game, match, or session has ended. He takes appropriate actions as describe in the sections below.

If the game session has not ended, the fun master moves to the beginning of the next song while being paused and goes to Step 1 - Turn Start, else all players go home.

### Game Round
A game round ends the first time one of the team reaches 10, 20, and 30 points. The fun master can change the song list at the end of a round.

### Game Match
A game match ends when one of the team reaches 40 points. The fun master can end a match earlier. The team with 40 points wins the match. The points are reset to 0. If more than two teams are playing, the fun master chooses the next two teams.

### Game Session
A game session ends when the fun master judges it is time to stop playing. All players who played the game win.

### Game Length
There is no time limit to a game of aRSD. The fun master can continue to pair teams until the players decide they have played enough. The number of songs in the lists can become a limit when playing over an extended period of time as repeating the same songs in a given game session is not recommended.

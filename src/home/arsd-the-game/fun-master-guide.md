Game Balance
Valid Song Title or Artist Assessment
Keeping the score as close as possible should be a constant objective of the fun master. Judging the players answers is a key element of doing so. Each answer should be judged based on the current game context and the player giving the answer. A team behind in points might only require a few valid words for an answer. A strong player should be judge more harshly than a player who has difficulty giving answers.

Countdowns
Song Start Countdown
When doing a 3-2-1 countdown before a turn starts, the fun master should try to produce a sense of suspense for the coming critical moment of when the song starts. The 3 often needs to be said the loudest to call the players to attention. Typically, chit chatting will stop and players will focus for the coming steps. Then 2-1 can be said in decreasing loudness resulting in a silence before the song starts.

Countdown Delays
When a player who has not given many answers tries to remember or a team is behind by many points, give them more than three seconds to give their answers to improve game balance. The fun master can either perform an elongated 3-2-1 countdown or delay the starting of the countdown.

Song Flow
When using random mode to play songs in a song list sometimes the same song can occur. The fun master should quickly declared the song invalid and start the next song without a countdown. If many seconds have elapsed or if the players are not silent, then a 3-2-1 countdown should be performed. The fun master can also skip duplicate songs if he notices them before un-pausing.

The fun master should pay attention to the players rection to different song types and artists. If songs of given type or artist are not popular, he should skip them. This has to be balanced with the number of songs available and the length of the play session.

Awarding Points
An efficient token scheme for points is to use poker chips. If multiple chip types are available, it is recommended to use 1 point chips, 5 points chips, and 10 points chips.

When saying the number of points awarded to a team for a song correct answer, the fun master should use an authoritative tone. When two points are awarded, the fun master should use an explosive and exclamative tone to highlight the risk taken by the player and the achievement accomplished.

Token Delivery
Tokens are given to teams when they score points. The fun master should deliver those points to the team with expressive gestures while the song is playing. The gestures should reflect the mood of the game. For example: respect, cheap win, cokky, dramatic, or any other emotion he feels. Dancing will delivering is recommended as it inspires the players to dance as well.

Throwing the tokens instead of hand delivering them can be fun the players can catch them each time. Sliding them across a long flat surface is also a good delivery mechanism.

Negative Points
Instead of tracking negative points for a team, it is simpler to give the matching points to the other team instead.

Double Points
At special moments during the game, the fun master can choose to double the points. Both win and lost points are doubled. This can be used, for example, to allow a trailing team to catch up.

Song List
Song List types
The list type defines what kind of songs are in a list:

Primary referenced as P: Well know energetic songs people like to hear, sing, or dance to. Can include a few very well known slow songs with lyrics people like to sing. The primary list songs should be loved and known by many players.
Secondary referenced as S: Known energetic songs people like to hear, sing, or dance to. Can include a few known slow songs with lyrics people like to sing.
Tertiary referenced as T: Less energetic and not as known songs than a S song list.
Songs which states either the title or the artist close to the beginning of the song should generally be avoided in aRSD. Especially songs which state the artist since it is easy for players to be certain that it is the right primary answer. The title is not as obvious and more difficult to be completely certain. Ideas are being investigate to handle such songs in future versions.

Songs should get going ideally within the first minute. In other words, avoid songs that have long slow starts.

Merge Song List Sub-type
A merge song list sub-type is a song list of type P, S, or T with an additional merge sub-type suffix of M.

 To be used in the game, merge song sub-lists are merged with a non-merge song list matching its type. For example: a PM merge song list will be merged with a P song list. 

Merge song list allow optional songs to be used based on context. For example: you have a P song list with rap songs but only want to include it in the game when some specific players are present. This list is PM: type primary with sub-type merge. It will be merged with a P list (non-merge) to be used in the game.

Merge song list must meet all criteria of their type. For example: a SM merge list should only include a few slower songs.

Song per Artist in a Song List
Type P and S song lists (merge or not) should have a similar number of songs from each artist. For example, 3 for Madonna, 2 from Queen, and 4 from Rihanna. A ball park is to be within +1/-1 from the average count. A similar count of songs by artist reduces predictability when the songs are played randomly thus making them harder to guess by players. A high diversity of artists in song lists also allows more players to participate more frequently which helps keeping them engaged.

Type T song lists have no artist distribution restrictions.

A-B-C Song List Qualifier
When running a game with multiple song lists of P, S, or T, you might want to call them A, B, and C where A match P, B matches S, and C matches T. If you have more than three lists, you can use sub-qualifiers like "A list rock" or "B list rap". Using A-B-C name qualifiers makes it easier for players to remember and associate content with a given list.

Song List Selection
To add diversity to the game when multiple song list are available, the fun master should switch between them. The song list selection can be used to balance the game as some teams will be strong with given lists. A simple scheme to trigger song list switches is when one of the team first reaches 10, 20, and 30 points but the fun master should feel free to improvise based on players taste at any time.

Song Lists Preparation
Prior to running a game the fun master should create song lists adapted to the specific players that will be present. He should use should merge song lists into primary lists and prune whichever songs might not be relevant. This time consumming activity can greatly improve the game experience of the players.

A simple wait of ensuring the players know song titles and artists is to ask them for a list of five to ten of their favorite song titles or artist prior to a game. When playing the fun master should pay attention to the different players taste for his next song lists preparation.

Quick Song Pausing
Bluetooth Speakers
Bluetooth speakers can have long pause delays. A typical setup of Bluetooth speaker with a smart phone has a delay longer than the minimum one second required to play aRSD. An alternative to using Bluetooth is an analog cable between the phone and the speaker when supported since there is no pause delay with such setup.

Spotify
Spotify multiple device support offers a work around to the previous Bluetooth problem for wireless pausing. If you can connect a Spotify device directly to your speakers, you can control the Spotify client using a different Spotify client. For example, a PS3 running Spotify can be connected to a speaker while using Spotify on a wireless smart phone to control the start and stop of songs without delay.
